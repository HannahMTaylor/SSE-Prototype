IEP Form (SE-7)


Individual Education Plan (IEP)

The Individual Education Program (IEP) is a written document developed for each eligible child with a disability. Part B regulation specifies the procedures the school must follow to develop, review, and revise the IEP for each child.

Section 13 of 17 - Annual Goals

Based on needs identified in the present levels section, include annual goal(s), including academic and functional goals designed to (a) meet the child/student’s needs that result from the child’s disability to enable the child to be  involved in and make progress in the general education curriculum, and (b) meet each of the child’s other educational needs that result from the child’s disability.

A measurable goal includes specific, observable, and quantifiable criteria that can be used to determine if the student has made progress.







For children with disabilities who take alternate assessments aligned to alternate achievement standards (in addition to the annual goals), include a description of benchmarks or short-term objectives. If not applicable enter N/A.







Description of (a) how the child’s progress toward meeting the annual goals will be measured, and (b) when periodic reports on the progress the child is making toward meeting the annual goals will be provided such as through the use of quarterly or other periodic reports, concurrent with the issuance of report cards.
